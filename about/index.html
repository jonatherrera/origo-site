About Origo

We started Origo because we believe great businesses deserve to be seen.

Where we started

Not just another agency origin story.

Before Origo, my wife Emily and I ran a health-focused food truck in a small town out in far West Texas. We called it Chop'd. Salads and wraps, soups and smoothies — the kind of food that small community didn't have yet. We found an old RV trailer that needed a lot of work, bought it for a good price, and Emily and her dad renovated the entire thing inside and out. New tires, new paint, new floors, new equipment. We brought it up to code and opened for business.

We ran Chop'd for about a year. We had a small, loyal customer base and very high ratings across social media and review platforms — reflecting the quality of the food, the service, and the experience. But the plan we'd laid out didn't give us enough runway to build the business to where it needed to be. It wasn't that the food truck was failing. It was that we didn't have the time and resources to keep building long enough for it to succeed the way it could have.

Going further back, to 2015, Emily and I were planting a church out of our living room. That work — ministry, community-building, showing up for the people around us — had been on our hearts for years. It's still central to how we live.

Even further back, to being a kid: I've always had fun with technology. Playing with code. Setting up servers. Building things that worked. In high school, a friend and I custom-coded two websites that were legitimately good enough to have been paid work. The interest in this field has always been there.

I started Origo because I'm wired for this life. Not "this digital work" specifically — this life of building. I love taking nothing and turning it into something. I love the chaos and the messiness of it. I love being part of watching something come into being. That drive has expressed itself through church planting, through the food truck, and now through Origo. The vehicle changes. The wiring doesn't.

Origo is what it looks like when the wiring for building meets eight years of digital experience and a conviction that good work — done on purpose, with care — still matters.

Why we exist

We're living through a strange moment.

AI is flooding the internet with content. Trust in institutions, media, and even the businesses we interact with is at a historic low. People are more skeptical than ever about what's real, what's credible, and who's worth their time and money.

In that environment, a strong, consistent, human digital presence isn't just nice to have — it's one of the most valuable things a business can build. It's the difference between being overlooked and being chosen.

That's what drives us. We want businesses that have earned their reputation to have a presence that reflects it. We want the right clients to find them, trust them, and choose them.

Mission
